Tsunamis are giant waves caused by earthquakes or volcanic eruptions under the sea.
Out in the depths of the ocean, tsunami waves do not dramatically increase in height.
But as the waves travel inland, they build up to higher and higher heights as the depth of the ocean decreases.
The speed of tsunami waves depends on ocean depth rather than the distance from the source of the wave.
Tsunami waves may travel as fast as jet planes over deep waters, only slowing down when reaching shallow waters.
While tsunamis are often referred to as tidal waves, this name is discouraged by oceanographers because tides have little to do with these giant waves.

  • Establish whether your home and other places you frequent are in tsunami hazard areas.

  • Know how high above sea level your street is and how far it is from the coast. Evacuation orders may be based on those figures.

  • Cobble together an emergency supplies kit.

  • Plan your escape and evacuation routes.

  • Practice evacuating.

  • Know your children's school evacuation plans and figure out how to get them.

1. If there's an earthquake while you are on the coast and indoors, drop, cover and hold on. If you're outside, stay away from falling objects.
2. When the shaking is over, move quickly to inland, higher ground. Go on foot if you can. Stay there until officials say it's all clear.
3. Bring your animals if you evacuate. Bring your preparedness kit.
4. Listen to official sources such as a National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration weather radio or the U.S.
5. Coast Guard emergency frequency station if a tsunami watch has been issued.

1. Tell family and friends you're OK.
2. Keep tuned in to official information sources or local media.
3. Don't assume the danger is over after the first wave. The next one might be bigger.
4. Call authorities if someone needs to be rescued.
5. Help people such as the elderly, infants and people with disabilities.
6. Stay away from disaster zones and out of buildings that have water around them.
7. Be cautious when re-entering buildings and cleaning up.
